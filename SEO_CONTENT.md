## What is Equipment Compatibility Checker?

The Equipment Compatibility Checker is a comprehensive web-based database tool that helps tattoo professionals verify whether their equipment components work together before making purchases or connecting gear. The tool covers four main equipment categories, tattoo machines (pen, rotary, coil), cartridge needle systems, power supplies, and grips/tubes, and cross-references real manufacturer specifications to flag incompatibilities.

The tool uses a JavaScript-based compatibility engine that checks connection types (RCA, clip cord, wireless), cartridge fit standards (universal vs proprietary), membrane types, and power requirements. It features fuzzy search via Fuse.js for partial name matching, a favorites system for frequently used equipment, result caching for performance, and social sharing capabilities. The tool includes six main tabs: Check Compatibility, Reverse Lookup, Build Your Setup, Compare Equipment, Suggest Gear, and FAQ & Troubleshooting.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: What does "Conditional" compatibility mean?

This means the components might work together, but with limitations. Common examples include needing an adapter (e.g., for RCA to clip cord), potential performance issues (like a low-amperage power supply with a demanding coil machine), or a safety concern (like a cartridge without a membrane). Always read the notes carefully.

### Q2: Why is my wireless machine showing as "Conditional" with a power supply?

Wireless machines have built-in batteries and do not require an external power supply or cables to operate. The tool flags this to prevent you from buying a power supply you don't need for that machine.

### Q3: What's the difference between RCA and Clip Cord connections?

RCA is a modern, stable, single-plug connection that provides consistent power. A Clip Cord is an older, two-pronged connection. While still functional, it's generally considered less reliable than RCA. Most modern power supplies offer outputs for both.

### Q4: What is a cartridge membrane and why is it important?

A safety membrane is a flexible barrier inside a tattoo cartridge that prevents ink, blood, and other fluids from flowing back into the machine's grip and motor. Using cartridges without a membrane can damage your machine, void its warranty, and creates a serious cross-contamination risk between clients.

### Q5: Can I use different brand cartridges with my machine?

Most modern rotary machines use the universal cartridge system, but we recommend checking specific drive bar tension. The tool verifies this by checking the cartridge's `compatible_machines` field against your machine's specifications.

### Q6: How is the compatibility database maintained?

The database contains real manufacturer specifications for machines, cartridges, power supplies, and grips. Users can suggest missing equipment through the "Suggest Gear" tab, which helps expand and update the database.

### Q7: Does the tool check voltage and amperage compatibility?

Yes, the tool checks power supply output amperage against machine requirements. Underpowered supplies can cause inconsistent needle depth and poor ink saturation. The notes section will flag any power-related concerns.

### Q8: Can I save my favorite equipment for quick access?

Yes, the tool includes a favorites system. Click the heart icon next to any equipment item to save it. You can then filter by favorites in the Reverse Lookup tab. Favorites are saved to your browser's local storage.

### Q9: How do I share my compatibility results?

Each compatibility result includes social sharing buttons for Twitter and Facebook. Click the share button to open a pre-populated post with your equipment details and compatibility status. You can also share full setup builds from the Build Your Setup tab.

### Q10: What browsers are supported?

The tool supports Chrome 90+, Firefox 88+, Safari 14+, Edge 90+, and their mobile counterparts. It requires JavaScript and HTML5. The tool uses local storage for preferences and favorites, and Intersection Observer for lazy loading.
